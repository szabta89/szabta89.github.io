I work as a principal engineer at Workday in a team of language and software engineers. Our goal is to build a next generation programming language and its accompanying tooling that will empower software development at Workday. I have a PhD in computer science from JGU Mainz. My PhD advisor was Sebastian Erdweg, and I was a member of the Programming Languages Research Group. My research interests are mostly centered around programming language design and implementation, static program analysis, and incremental computations.

Prior to joining Workday, I have worked in the language engineering team of itemis. Over the years, we have built a number of domain-specific languages and their accompanying tooling using MPS in various applications domains.

I have obtained my B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees in computer science from the Technical University of Budapest, where I have worked in the Fault Tolerant Systems Research Group.
